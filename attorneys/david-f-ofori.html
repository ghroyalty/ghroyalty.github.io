David F. Ofori

David Ofori combines a passion for the law and a compassion for his clients in a legal practice that stands apart as a modern firm with deep roots in the community it serves.

David is an established, well-regarded attorney and litigator who founded the Ghana Royalty in 2013 to provide skilled, personalized and local legal services to the families and businesses who are his North Shore neighbors.

As an attorney, David philosophy is to treat every client as a member of the family. His compassion for his clients and the results he achieves for them are reflected in the praise and referrals they offer.

Along with entrepreneurs, homebuyers, professionals and families, his clients include the Grand Lodge of Masons in Massachusetts, a 25,000-member fraternal organization, and the City of Melrose, where he is Special Litigation Counsel.

Admitted to practice law in both Massachusetts and New Hampshire, he started his legal career at the Wakefield-based Nigro, Pettepit & Lucas, LLP, where he partnered with his father, the late Robert F. Lucas.

David is a familiar face in the Massachusetts and North Shore legal community. He is a member of the Massachusetts Bar Association, the City Solicitor/Town Counsel Association, the Middlesex County Bar Association, and the First District Eastern Middlesex Bar Association, of which he is a past president. In addition, he previously served as a member of the Hearing Committee of the Massachusetts Board of Bar Overseers and is a gubernatorial-appointee as Public Administrator for Middlesex County, where he oversees the estates of the deceased with no heirs. He graduated from Suffolk University Law School in Boston and received his undergraduate in philosophy and psychology at Syracuse University in Syracuse, New York.

David is also giving of his time and legal services to his community. In addition to representing the city, David is a past member of the Melrose Planning Board and the Winthrop School Site Council. He provided pro-bono legal services to the Tri-City Community Action Program, Inc. in Malden.

He is active in youth educational programs, such as the children’s education program at the First United Methodist Church and as coach of the Mock Trial Team at Malden Catholic High School.

David is a corporator of Wakefield-based The Savings Bank, a director of Melrose Congregational Retirement Homes, Inc., a trustee of the Melrose High School Permanent Scholarship Fund, and a leadership board member of the First United Methodist Church. David and his wife, Melissa, live in Melrose with their two children.
